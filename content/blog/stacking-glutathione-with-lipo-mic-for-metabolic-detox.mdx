###### *“Detox” is one of the most misused words in wellness. Here’s what it actually means when clinicians do it — and why these two compounds are central to the protocol.*

##### **What Detox Actually Means Biologically**

To start off with some clarity on what "detox" really means biologically before getting into the scientific explanation - detoxification is the liver's normal ongoing process of detecting, chemically bonding to and neutralizing potentially toxic substances in our environment - such as pollutants, metabolic byproducts, oxidative stress indicators, etc. - then removing those potential toxins from our bodies.

However, there are essentially two ways this biological detoxification process fails. First, oxidative stress overloads the liver and thus inhibits the liver from functioning properly. Second, even if the liver is functioning normally, the liver still needs the appropriate nutrients to be able to process and remove fats through the bile pathway. Neither of these problems have been addressed in most detox programs. Both issues have been addressed by Glutathione and Lipo-MIC.

##### **Glutathione: The Body's Primary Endogenous Antioxidant**

Glutathione is the body's first line of defense against free radical damage caused by reactive oxygen species (ROS). ROS attacks cellular membranes and causes lipid oxidation; therefore, glutathione serves as an antioxidant in all phases of the liver detoxification processes, primarily during Phase II where toxins are conjugated for removal.¹

A 2025 study demonstrated that glutathione therapy consistently lowered serum alanine transaminase (ALT) levels - indicative of better liver function - and decreased oxidative stress markers in individuals with impaired liver function.² Mechanism: glutathione acts to scavenge free radicals before damaging liver cells. Additionally, glutathione provides necessary cofactors to the enzymes used to metabolize and excrete metabolic waste products by the liver.

Practically speaking - when the body experiences high oxidative loads due to factors like poor sleep, diet rich in processed foods/alcohol/environmental exposures/metabolic waste generated by losing large amounts of fat rapidly, endogenous glutathione levels will deplete much quicker than can be replaced. Injectable glutathione bypasses the limited absorption capacity of orally ingested glutathione and directly raises levels of glutathione within tissues.¹

##### **Lipo-MIC: The Liver's Logistics Crew**

While Glutathione helps manage oxidative stress, Lipo-MIC addresses another significant issue related to fat accumulation in the liver.

MIC represents Methionine-Inositol-Choline; therefore, Lipo-MIC represents a combination of methionine, inositol, and choline. These compounds work synergistically to facilitate efficient hepatic fat metabolism. Methionine is an essential amino acid required for production of phosphatidylcholine, which is the major molecule the liver utilizes to encapsulate and export fats. Inositol aids insulin signaling and lipid transport. Choline is necessary for formation of VLDL - the method by which the liver packages and transports fats out of hepatocytes and into circulation for utilization as energy.

If insufficient methionine and choline are available to assist in fat exportation, fats that were previously metabolized by the liver remain accumulated in hepatocytes rather than being exported; this condition is known clinically as hepatic steatosis. Therefore, Lipo-MIC combined with B-12 provides the raw materials necessary for the liver to complete exportation of fats efficiently so that fat does not accumulate where it should not.³
