###### *The medication is working. So why does week four feel like running on fumes? The answer is biochemical — and so is the solution.*

##### **The Crash Nobody Warned You About**

There are few drugs in today's pharmaceutical market that can claim to cause such dramatic weight loss. In clinical trials for Tirzepatide, participants experienced average losses of 20-22% of their total body weight. These numbers have historically been possible only through bariatric surgery. Tirzepatide works; it suppresses hunger and promotes fat loss, which motivates many patients initially.

However, some time around weeks three to five after initiating treatment, something changes. Energy -- typically expected to follow significant weight loss -- does not appear. Instead, there is a growing fatigue (the type that even sleep cannot fully alleviate and that caffeine cannot even partially address). While this may seem like an issue with the medication itself, it is simply indicative of a resource constraint among your mitochondria.

##### **The Resource Problem Among Mitochondria**

The reason why Tirzepatide causes a resource problem among mitochondria occurs because when Tirzepatide causes an initial dramatic reduction in food intake due to its appetite-suppressing effects, the body rapidly increases fat mobilization. This is exactly what Tirzepatide was intended to do. However, the process of using the mobilized fat as fuel requires massive quantities of NAD+, which is a coenzyme required by your mitochondria to operate the electron transport chain and generate ATP.1

The problem here lies in the fact that the quantity of dietary NAD+ precursors (niacin, tryptophan, etc.) available to the body is drastically reduced when you significantly decrease your daily caloric intake. Therefore, while the demand for NAD+ is increasing due to increased fat mobilization, the supply of NAD+ is decreasing. As a result, there develops a mitochondrial energy bottleneck that is evidenced by the fatigue described by many Tirzepatide patients.1

Again, this fatigue is not caused by the medication itself. Rather, it is merely a secondary consequence of the metabolic state created by the medication.

##### **Understanding What NAD+ Does at the Cellular Level**

At the cellular level, NAD+ is fundamental to producing cellular energy. Each molecule of glucose or fat that your cells metabolize passes through pathways that require NAD+ as a cofactor. Therefore, when your cells contain adequate quantities of NAD+, your mitochondria can successfully complete the electron transport chain and maximize ATP output.2 On the other hand, if your cells contain low levels of NAD+, then your mitochondria will slow down the completion of the electron transport chain and you will experience decreased ATP output -- which is manifested as fatigue.2

Beyond its role in providing energy for your cells, NAD+ also activates sirtuins, a family of proteins involved in longevity signaling, metabolic regulation and cellular stress response. Additionally, NAD+ provides support for DNA repair mechanisms and helps to mitigate oxidative stress caused by the acceleration of fat metabolism during rapid weight loss. Since all of these systems are experiencing increased demand simultaneously during rapid weight loss, supplementation with NAD+ is essential to provide them with the necessary resources.2

##### **A Clinician’s Viewpoint on Supplemental Use of NAD+**

As more clinicians continue to work at the intersection of GLP-1 therapies and metabolic medicine, a growing number are beginning to incorporate NAD+ support as part of their standard protocols for Tirzepatide patients -- not as an after-thought, but as a pro-active approach to maintaining mitochondrial function throughout the weight-loss phase.3

From a clinician’s perspective, it is clear that Tirzepatide performs one primary function (managing appetite and insulin signaling) whereas NAD+ performs another (providing your cells with the necessary cofactors needed to convert mobilized fat into usable energy). The two functions are not redundant; they complement each other.

Many patients who report on this combination describe improved and sustained energy levels within one-to-two weeks of starting NAD+ supplements. Patients report having clearer mental focus, less fatigue during periods of high caloric restriction, and generally report a smoother experience overall.3
