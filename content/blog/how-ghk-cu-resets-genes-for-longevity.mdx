###### *Peer-reviewed data is revealing something remarkable about a copper peptide your body already makes — and why its decline may be central to how you age.*

##### Genomic Time Machine

The GHK-Cu molecule in our bodies can stimulate a range of cellular processes, potentially slowing the rate of aging. Recent scientific findings suggest that this molecule affects many of the thousands of genetic signals that regulate the various systems within our bodies. Our body produces the GHK-Cu molecule and has been producing it since we developed in utero. While there are now numerous published studies demonstrating the effects of GHK-Cu on human physiology, researchers continue to seek answers regarding the mechanisms through which GHK-Cu exerts these effects and how its production decreases with each passing year.

##### Resetting Aging Processes

Data obtained using genomic arrays shows that GHK-Cu significantly increases the expression of over 4,000 human genes. These include genes involved in the protection against oxidative damage, inflammation, and DNA repair. Of particular interest is the fact that GHK-Cu influences the regulation of 47 genes related to DNA repair; GHK-Cu also decreases the expression of five genes related to DNA repair. Although few interventions focus on regulating multiple processes simultaneously, GHK-Cu appears to reset the regulatory processes of nearly four thousand genes. The link between the GHK-Cu and DNA repair is based upon its activation of SIRT1. SIRT1 has long been recognized as a key factor in longevity. It has been targeted by scientists for the last decade due to its role in controlling gene expression, cellular metabolism, and cellular responses to stress. GHK-CU provides evidence that SIRT1 is activated by GHK-CU and that it activates SIRT1.

##### Skin and Liver Studies Provide Clearer Proof of Concept

While evidence supporting the therapeutic application of GHK-Cu has emerged from several areas of research, the initial discovery occurred when a scientist isolated GHK as an activity in human albumin that stimulated older human liver tissue to produce protein similar to younger liver tissue. Since that time, over 50 additional years of research have identified other ways in which GHK-Cu promotes youthfulness in both human skin and liver. Human clinical trials demonstrate that topical applications of GHK-CU result in significant improvements in skin elasticity, firmness, density, and thickness, while simultaneously improving wrinkling (fine lines and coarse wrinkles) and hyperpigmentation (mottled pigmentation). GHK-Cu induces changes in skin cells at the cellular level including stimulation of keratinocyte proliferation and induction of collagen synthesis and extracellular matrix remodeling. Formulations containing GHK-Cu combined with Tretinoin and Niacinamide provide additive benefits for skin regeneration. Some studies indicate up to a 70% increase in dermal collagen density following regular use of products containing GHK-CU.

##### Implications for Longevity Strategies

The significance of GHK-Cu cannot be overstated. It offers scientists and practitioners interested in longevity strategies an opportunity to utilize a substance that has demonstrated efficacy in activating biological processes with documented safety profiles and extensive peer-reviewed literature. Furthermore, GHK-CU demonstrates potential for being used as a foundational approach to promote longevity by addressing biological processes early on. Most approaches to promoting longevity fall into two categories: those that occur upstream (lifestyle modifications such as sleep and diet), and those that occur downstream (responses to injury/damage). Utilizing GHK-CU would represent a third category of intervention: altering gene expression. Therefore, decreasing levels of endogenously produced GHK is likely associated with aging. However, supplementation with GHK-CU will restore a signal that your body depends upon. Your body is familiar with GHK-CU. The science continues to validate how much GHK-CU has done for your body over time.
