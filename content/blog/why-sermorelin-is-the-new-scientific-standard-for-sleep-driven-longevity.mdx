###### *You optimize your training, your nutrition, your supplement stack. But most men aren’t optimizing the eight hours where the real repair happens.*

You may be training like crazy; you may be fueling your body with optimal nutrition; you may have invested heavily in your supplements. However, most men will never optimize their bodies' "repair phase" – the eight hours per day when actual repair occurs.

##### The Window Most Men Neglect

Growth Hormones are not released based on demand. It is not released because of your workout routine or the amount of protein you consume. About 70% of the daily GH secretions happen during the stage three non-rapid eye movement (NREM) sleep period, also known as the slow wave sleep (SWS). Additionally, the GH pulses that occur during SWS happen predominantly in the first cycle of the night.

By the time most men reach their thirties, the quality of slow-wave sleep and the amplitude of nocturnal GH pulses have begun to significantly decrease. This decrease continues until approximately forty-five years old. The effects are not abstract; they include decreased recovery times, loss of lean muscle mass, loss of strength and elasticity in connective tissues, and a brain that is unable to clear metabolic waste products from the brain as quickly as it should.

Sermorelin addresses this at the base level – not by artificially inducing sleep – rather by allowing for deep sleep to be productive again.

##### GHRH, Sleep, and the Lost Pulse

Sermorelin is a 29-amino acid analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH), which is the signal sent from the hypothalamus to the pituitary gland prior to sleep to begin GH secretion. The essential aspect: Endogenously produced GHRH has the greatest pulsatility directly synchronized with SWS. When taken before bedtime, sermorelin is intended to functionally synchronize with the natural pulse and rhythm of GHRH – not suppress it.

With aging, there is a progressive reduction in the amplitude of these GHRH pulses, resulting in a diminished responsiveness of the pituitary gland. As a result, the GH release pattern becomes flattened as compared to what would be seen in a younger, healthier endocrine system. Essentially, sermorelin resets the signal that prompts the pituitary gland to release GH similar to what would occur in a younger, healthier individual.

##### The Glymphatic System: Your Brain's Over-Night Waste Removal Crew

Researchers at the University of Rochester identified the glymphatic system — a glial dependent waste removal pathway within the brain — in 2013. In essence, the glymphatic system is responsible for removing waste products from within the brain while we sleep. The glymphatic system is most actively involved during slow-wave sleep, specifically during stages III and IV NREM sleep.

During this time, cerebrospinal fluid flows through the brain tissue, washing away metabolic byproducts such as beta-amyloid, a protein associated with cognitive decline when allowed to accumulate.

There is a direct correlation between Sermorelin and enhancement of slow-wave sleep quality and stimulation of the glymphatic system. GH-deficient men demonstrate decreased duration and frequency of slow-wave sleep as well as increased nighttime awakenings. GH replacement resulted in measurable improvements in both aspects.

If a man does not achieve high-quality, prolonged periods of slow-wave sleep, his brain is not able to complete its overnight maintenance program. Therefore, Sermorelin represents much more than recovery; it represents maintenance of neural processes.
